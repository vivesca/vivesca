# Munger in Long Conversation — Patterns Under Pressure

Tag legend: [VERBATIM] = quoted from a transcript or video. [PARAPHRASE-FROM-RECAP] = paraphrased by a journalist or summary author.

---

## 1. The "I have nothing to add" pattern (Berkshire)

The default mode at Berkshire is silence — Munger sits next to Buffett through 6+ hours of Q&A and contributes one or two lines per session. The phrase becomes so iconic that at the 2010 Burlington Northern shareholder meeting, Buffett produces a cardboard cutout of Munger and plays a recording of "I have nothing to add" — and the crowd roars. The contrast between his Berkshire silence and his Daily Journal torrent IS the data: at Berkshire the bar to speak is "I'd actively contradict Warren or sharpen the point." At the Daily Journal there is no Warren, so the bar disappears.

> "Buffett speaks in long paragraphs; Mr. Munger speaks in zingers and, most often, simply says: 'I have nothing to add.'" [PARAPHRASE-FROM-RECAP] — Jason Zweig, https://jasonzweig.com/a-fireside-chat-with-charlie-munger/

> "Charlie was never truly just offering nothing to add — that was never true. Charlie always had plenty to add." [PARAPHRASE-FROM-RECAP] — Gary Carmell tribute, https://www.garycarmell.com/i-have-nothing-to-add-charlie-munger/

## 2. Refusals and dismissals — the negative space

> "I think I'll duck that one." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023, on 2024 presidential election. https://www.kingswell.io/p/charlie-munger-q-and-a-2023-daily

> "What the hell do I know about semiconductors?" [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023. https://steadycompounding.com/transcript/djco23/

> "I don't use Twitter. So I'm not a good judge on that subject." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023. https://steadycompounding.com/transcript/djco23/

> "I'm not sure I am any good answering that kind of a question." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023, on climate/insurance. https://steadycompounding.com/transcript/djco23/

> "I don't have the faintest idea what's going to happen short term." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023. https://www.kingswell.io/p/charlie-munger-q-and-a-2023-daily

> "Remember, that I did not make my fortune such as it is by predicting macro economic changes better than other people." [VERBATIM] — Caltech 2020. https://www.rev.com/transcripts/investor-charlie-munger-caltech-interview-transcript-december-16

> "I have a way of handling a lot of problems. I put them on what I call my too hard pile and I just leave them there." [VERBATIM] — Caltech 2020.

Pattern: refuses on (1) politics, (2) outside his circle, (3) short-term predictions, (4) climate/insurance technical. Does NOT refuse on: ethics, incentive design, life advice, China, crypto, his own mistakes.

## 3. How he handles pushback or disagreement

Two gears, no in-between.

**Clean concession when caught (DJ 2023, on his own past leverage):**
> "You're right. I used a little on my way up and so did Warren, by the way... If the opportunity is great enough, the logical answer is 100%. Or maybe 200%. The young man knows the rules and the old man knows the exceptions." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023. https://www.kingswell.io/p/charlie-munger-q-and-a-2023-daily

**Total refusal of legitimacy (DJ 2023, on crypto):**
> "I think the people who oppose my position are idiots... I don't think there are good arguments against my position." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

> "I don't think there is a rational argument against my position... When you're dealing with something as awful as crypto shit, it's just unspeakable." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

Selector: empirical question with defensible counter → concession. Moral/incentive question where he sees rot → opposing view labelled idiotic.

## 4. Position changes — the highest-signal section

### 4a. Alibaba/China — narrow mistake, broad view held

**Pre-shift:** Munger held large Alibaba positions through Daily Journal Corp, expanded through 2021. Framing: Chinese internet leadership at a discount.

**Post-shift (DJ 2023):**
> "I regard Alibaba as one of the worst mistakes I ever made... I got charmed with the idea of their position in the Chinese internet and I didn't stop to realize that it's still a goddamn retailer." [VERBATIM] — https://www.kingswell.io/p/charlie-munger-q-and-a-2023-daily

What shifted: realization Alibaba was a retailer, not a moated tech platform. Note he did NOT pull back on China generally:
> "I would argue that the chances of a big confrontation from China have gone down, not up, because of what happened in Ukraine... You can buy better, stronger companies at a cheaper valuation in China than you can in the United States." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

The position change is *narrow* (Alibaba misclassification) not *broad* (China as category). When admitting error, he isolates the lesson tightly.

### 4b. Cigar butts → compounders (the Costco arc)

**Pre-shift (Graham orthodoxy):**
> "I'm enough of a big Ben Graham follower… so if something is really cheap, even though it's a crappy company, I'm willing to consider buying it." [PARAPHRASE-FROM-RECAP] — Acquired Oct 2023, summary at https://www.mymoneyblog.com/charlie-munger-acquired-podcast-interview-transcript.html

**The Graham critique:**
> "Buying those cheap, cigar-butt stocks was a snare and a delusion." [VERBATIM] — Jason Zweig fireside. https://jasonzweig.com/a-fireside-chat-with-charlie-munger/

> "Ben Graham actually made more than half of all the money he made in his life out of one stock — and that stock was GEICO, which was a great business... what he taught wasn't the way he got rich himself." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Post-shift (DJ 2023, on Costco):**
> "As long as Costco keeps the faith with its strong culture and extreme low mark-up policy, I don't see any stopping it. The trouble with Costco is that it's 40x earnings, but except for that it's a perfect damn company and it has a marvelous future." [VERBATIM]

> "I'm a total addict. And I'm never gonna sell a share." [VERBATIM]

> "I don't like even looking for exits. I'm looking for holds." [VERBATIM] — https://www.benzinga.com/news/management/25/05/45248740/charlie-munger-says-the-secret-is-to-find-costcos-not-good-exits

What shifted: the GEICO observation. Graham preached margin-of-safety arithmetic but actually got rich from one great compounder he held through. The empirical record of his own teacher contradicted his teacher's stated method.

### 4c. Crypto — never moved, only escalated (the negative case)

> "I think it's rat poison." [VERBATIM] — Berkshire 2018. https://buffett.cnbc.com/video/2018/05/05/munger-on-trading-cryptocurrencies-its-just-dementia.html

> "It's just dementia." [VERBATIM] — Berkshire 2018.

> "It's like some venereal disease... I admire the Chinese for banning it. I think they were right and we were wrong to allow it." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2022. https://cointelegraph.com/news/crypto-is-like-venereal-disease-and-should-be-banned-charlie-munger

> "The crypto shit. It's worthless, it's no good, it's crazy, it will do nothing but harm." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

> "It isn't even slightly stupid, it's massively stupid." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

Escalation curve: rat poison (2018) → venereal disease (2022) → "massively stupid"/"shit" (2023). When Munger does NOT change his mind, the language gets sharper, not softer. **The intensity of contempt is the time-signal of unchanged conviction.** Note: "rat poison squared" is Buffett's escalation of Munger's "rat poison" — Munger seeded it but Buffett squared it.

## 5. Munger-isms in dialogue — verbatim sightings

**Show me the incentive** — origin is 1995 Harvard speech, not DJ. Late-period he runs the principle, not the slogan:
> "I gave those shares to the company to use in compensating the employees. I learned that trick... from the guy at BYD." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Envy:**
> "Envy is such a stupid thing to have because you can't possibly have any fun with that particular sin. Who in the hell ever had any fun in envy? What good could envy possibly do for you?" [VERBATIM] — DJ 2019. https://www.mymoneyblog.com/charlie-munger-daily-journal-annual-meeting-2019-full-video-transcript.html

> "The world is not driven by greed. It's driven by envy... I have conquered envy in my own life. I don't envy anybody. I don't give a damn what someone else has." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2019.

**Viper down my shirt-front:**
> "I'd rather throw a viper down my shirt front than hire a compensation consultant." [VERBATIM] — Berkshire 2004. https://buffett.cnbc.com/video/2004/05/01/munger-i-would-rather-throw-a-viper-down-my-shirtfront-than-hire-a-compensation-consultant.html

**All I want to know is where I'm going to die** — Munger attributes this to "the rustic"; lives mostly in books, rare in transcripts. [PARAPHRASE-FROM-RECAP] https://www.poorcharliesalmanack.com/all_i_want_to_know.php

**Not supposed to be easy:**
> "It's not going to be that damn easy." [VERBATIM] — Acquired Oct 2023.
> "Capitalism is not easy. Even if you're a genius like Musk is in some ways, there's always some little BYD that comes out and does better." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Iron rule of life:**
> "The iron rule of life is that everybody struggles." [VERBATIM] — Kingswell tribute. https://www.kingswell.io/p/the-late-great-charlie-munger

**Soldier through:**
> "If you soldier through, you can get through almost anything... If you have to walk through the streets, crying for a few hours a day as part of the soldiering through — go ahead and cry away. But you can't quit." [VERBATIM]

**Just not crazy:**
> "If you're just not crazy, you have a big advantage over 95% of the people." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.
> "If you're patient and a gratification-deferrer, in addition to being not crazy, then it's practically a cinch." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Dumb is forever:**
> "There's an old saying, dumb is forever." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Edge of competency:**
> "The single most important thing if you want to avoid a lot of stupid errors is knowing where you're competent and where you aren't, knowing the edge of your own competency." [VERBATIM] — Caltech 2020.
> "Knowing what you don't know is more useful than being brilliant." [VERBATIM] — Zweig fireside.

## 6. Spontaneous analogies — what he reaches for

**Biology — most reached-for in 2020–2023:**
> "Over the longterm, the companies of America behave more like biology than they do anything else... all the individuals die and so do all the species." [VERBATIM] — Caltech 2020.

> "The business world is very much like the physical world where all the animals die in the course of improving all the species... All the animals die and, eventually, all the species die." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

> "Look at biology. Produces a very advanced creature like us... but it does it by killing everybody off in brutal competition." [VERBATIM] — Acquired Oct 2023.

**Animal-aggression for geopolitics:**
> "It's like poking a bear in the nose with a sharp stick. It's not smart." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023, on Jack Ma's pre-Ant-Group speech.

**Tightrope walker for circle of competence:**
> "Think of somebody who's been a professional tightrope walker for 20 years – and has survived. He couldn't survive... unless he knows exactly what he knows and what he doesn't know." [VERBATIM] — Zweig fireside.

**Heroin for active trading:**
> "If you take the modern world where people are trying to teach you how to come in and trade actively in stocks. Well I regard that as roughly equivalent to trying to induce a bunch of young people to start off on heroin." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2019.

**Las Vegas / slaughter of innocents for derivatives:**
> "It's like the slaughter of the innocents. It makes the people who run Las Vegas seem like good people." [VERBATIM] — Zweig fireside.

**Confucius (cited as authority):**
> "Real knowledge is knowing the extent of one's ignorance." [VERBATIM] — Zweig fireside (Munger attributing to Confucius).

**Demosthenes for self-deception:**
> "What people wish is what they believe." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023, Munger citing as 2,000+ years old.

**FDR's wheelchair for self-pity:**
> "Whenever I feel sad about being in a wheelchair, I think that Roosevelt ran the whole damn country for twelve years in a wheelchair." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Climbing one inch at a time:**
> "You climb as hard as you can by just advancing one inch at a time. That's the secret of life." [VERBATIM] — DJ 2023.

**Costco parking spaces — engineering specificity:**
> "They really did sell cheaper than anybody else in America and they did it in big, efficient stores. All the parking spaces were 10 feet wide instead of eight or nine feet." [VERBATIM] — Acquired Oct 2023.

The Carl-Braun-style instinct: don't say "they obsess over operations", give the literal foot measurement.

**Notably underweighted in late conversations:**
- Poker — heavily present in 1990s speeches but rarer in DJ 2017–2023
- Cicero / Galileo — present in books but less frequent in spontaneous Q&A this period
- Carl Braun's communication rules — structural influence on his prose but not a quoted reflex
- Genghis Khan — rare in primary transcripts within budget

## 7. Things he refused to discuss — the negative space

1. **Presidential / partisan politics.** "I think I'll duck that one." [DJ 2023]
2. **Short-term market direction.** "I don't have the faintest idea what's going to happen short term." [DJ 2023]
3. **Domains outside his circle.** "What the hell do I know about semiconductors?" [DJ 2023]
4. **Climate-and-insurance technical questions.** "I'm not sure I am any good answering that kind of a question." [DJ 2023]
5. **Macro forecasting as a method.** [Caltech 2020]
6. **Specific stock predictions** (he'll discuss disclosed holdings but won't name future buys).
7. **Personal humility theatre.** "In my whole life, nobody has ever accused me of being humble." — false humility as another form of dishonesty. [Kingswell tribute]

What he is happy to discuss even when uninvited: incentives, envy, denial, his own mistakes, China geopolitics, ethics in compensation, the rot in venture capital and crypto, the shape of capitalism over decades.

## 8. Cross-source synthesis — six conversational moves

1. **Curt refusal with a competence reason**, never a "let me think about it" hedge. The refusal is its own answer.
2. **Concession on empirical asymmetry**, refusal-of-legitimacy on moral/incentive asymmetry. Two gears.
3. **Mistake admission is narrow, not broad.** Alibaba was a retailer he misclassified — not "China was a mistake."
4. **Conviction-without-update intensifies the language.** Crypto: rat poison → venereal disease → "shit". The escalation IS the signal of unchanged conviction.
5. **Analogy reflex is biology-first, animal-aggression-second, ancient-authority-third.** 2020s Munger reaches for biology more than 1990s Munger.
6. **Engineering precision as proof of seriousness.** "10 feet wide instead of eight or nine" — Costco parking spaces. Specific measurement standing in for "they actually run the place."

The constant: **structure of the answer is selected by the structure of the question**. He has a refusal tone, a concession tone, an escalation tone, and an analogy tone, and he switches between them deterministically based on question type. **That switching is itself the imitable thing.**
