## 3. Reproducible Doctrines (≥3 cross-talk instances — deep beliefs)

- **Inversion ("Invert, always invert").** Munger credits **Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi**. Recurs Talks 2, 5, 8, 11. [PRIMARY] Source: [fs.blog Psychology](https://fs.blog/great-talks/psychology-human-misjudgment/).
- **Latticework of mental models.** Verbatim Talk 2 (1994 USC): "You've got to have models in your head. And you've got to array your experience — both vicarious and direct — on this latticework of models." [PRIMARY] Source: [fs.blog Worldly Wisdom](https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/). Recurs Talks 2, 3, 5, 9.
- **Lollapalooza effects.** Verbatim Talk 11 definition: "Lollapalooza Tendency — The Tendency to Get Extreme Consequences from Confluences of Psychology Tendencies Acting in Favor of a Particular Outcome." [PRIMARY] Recurs Talks 2, 3, 5, 11.
- **Circle of competence.** Talk 2 (1994 USC): "Every person is going to have a circle of competence. And it's going to be very hard to advance that circle." [PRIMARY] Recurs Talks 2, 3, 5, 6, 9.
- **Opportunity cost as universal benchmark.** Talk 2 — only buy Y if it beats your best alternative X. [PRIMARY — paraphrase across editions.] Recurs Talks 2, 5, 6, 9. Berkshire's formal allocation rule.
- **Checklist approach.** Talk 11 explicitly framed as a 25-item misjudgment checklist. [PRIMARY] Recurs Talks 2, 5, 11.
- **Iron prescription / persevere without hope.** Talk 11 verbatim: "It is not necessary to hope in order to persevere." Munger attributes line to **William the Silent** (William of Orange, 16c). [PRIMARY] Recurs Talks 2, 10, 11.
- **Man-with-a-hammer syndrome.** Talk 2 (1994 USC) verbatim: "It's like the old saying, 'To the man with only a hammer, every problem looks like a nail.'" [PRIMARY — saying predates Munger; he popularised it.] Recurs Talks 2, 3, 5, 9, 11.

## 4. Doctrines flagged as shallow / one-time framings

- **"Fish where the fish are."** Strong in Daily Journal/Berkshire Q&A; only glancing in the 11 prepared talks. **Flag: doctrine-by-Q&A, not doctrine-by-essay.** Belongs to the speeches/interviews swarm member, not WRITINGS.
- **"Tell me where I'm going to die so I never go there."** [PARAPHRASE] Pithy summary not pinned verbatim to a specific talk; appears in Q&A more than prepared text.
- **"Cocoa Puffs."** Q&A slang only; not in the 11 talks. Treat as informal, not doctrinal.
- **Talk 4's specific "five-step" framework.** Appears once and is not re-cited; the lattice + inversion components within it recur, but the numbered steps don't.

## 6. Recommended Reading List (intellectual genealogy)

Sources: Poor Charlie's Almanack front-matter; [fs.blog list](https://fs.blog/charlie-munger-recommended-books/); [thewaystowealth.com](https://www.thewaystowealth.com/charlie-munger-recommended-books/).

**Confirmed core list:**
- **Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion** — Cialdini (foundational source for Talk 11; Munger gifted Cialdini Berkshire stock)
- **The Selfish Gene; The Blind Watchmaker** — Dawkins
- **Guns, Germs, and Steel; The Third Chimpanzee** — Diamond
- **Ice Age** — John & Mary Gribbin (frequently misattributed to Macdougall)
- **How the Mind Works; The Language Instinct** — Pinker
- **Three Scientists and Their Gods** — Robert Wright
- **Andrew Carnegie** — Joseph Frazier Wall (Peter Krass version also cited)
- **The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin** — book's title echoes *Poor Richard's Almanack*; Franklin is Munger's chosen role model
- **Models of My Life** — Herbert Simon (bounded rationality)
- **Deep Simplicity** — John Gribbin
- **F.I.A.S.C.O.** — Frank Partnoy (Wall Street pathology)
- **Genome** — Matt Ridley
- **Living within Limits** — Garrett Hardin
- **Titan: John D. Rockefeller** — Ron Chernow
- **The Wealth and Poverty of Nations** — David Landes
- **Only the Paranoid Survive** — Andy Grove
- **Getting to Yes** — Fisher & Ury
- **Faraday, Maxwell, and the Electromagnetic Field** — Forbes & Mahon
- **How the Scots Invented the Modern World** — Arthur Herman

**What the list reveals about HOW Munger thinks:**
- Heavy weighting toward **biographies of allocator-builders** (Carnegie, Rockefeller, Franklin, Buffett, Grove). Munger studies operators, not theorists.
- Heavy weighting toward **evolutionary biology + cognitive science** (Dawkins, Pinker, Ridley, Cialdini) — substrate for the 25-bias list.
- **No pure finance textbooks.** Conspicuously absent.
- **No philosophy primary sources.** Cicero, Demosthenes, Confucius are absorbed via aphorism, not study.

## 7. Recurring Quoted Authorities (authorities Munger leans on by name in prepared talks)

- **Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi** — "Invert, always invert." (Talks 2, 11)
- **Benjamin Franklin** — role model; biography in reading list; book title echoes *Poor Richard's*
- **Cicero** — historical-perspective quote ("not to know what happened before you were born is to remain always a child"). Talk 10 USC Gould 2007.
- **Demosthenes** — "Nothing is so easy as to deceive oneself; for what we wish, that we readily believe." Talk 11 anchor for denial/overoptimism.
- **Confucius** — virtue and learning; Talks 5, 10. (Most quotations actually in Q&A — flag.)
- **Einstein** — "everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler." Talks 2, 5, 9.
- **Carl Braun** — engineer at C.F. Braun construction; **5W communication rule** (who/what/where/when/why). Talks 2, 5.
- **Lord Acton** — "Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely." Talk 9 (UCSB 2003).
- **B.F. Skinner** — operant conditioning underwrites Tendency #1. Talk 11.
- **Charles Darwin** — Munger's intellectual hero for **objectivity discipline** (actively seeking disconfirming evidence). Talks 5, 11.
- **Adam Smith** — invoked in Talk 9 (economics).
- **J.K. Galbraith** — *The Great Crash 1929*; source of "bezzle." Talks 8, 9.
- **William the Silent** — "It is not necessary to hope in order to persevere." Talk 11 iron prescription.

## 8. Notes / caveats

- All 25 tendencies cross-verified against two independent transcripts.
- "Iron Prescription" verbatim + William the Silent attribution confirmed; "Invert, always invert" + Jacobi attribution confirmed in Talk 11.
- **Reading list is not single-source canonical.** Different editions adjust it; the Almanack 2005/06/08 front-matter is the most reliable subset. Wider lists (50+) curate from talks/interviews.
- **WRITINGS scope only.** Daily Journal + Berkshire annual meetings — where many Mungerisms ("fish where the fish are," "cocoa puffs") actually originate — are out of scope; covered in 02-conversations.md.
