# Mappa — Life Areas Map

*Mappa* (Latin: map/chart) — a personal diagnostic for life balance. Not self-help, not aspirational — a systems check. When something feels off but you can't name it, scan this.

**Purpose:** Reference skill consulted by other skills (kairos, daily, eow, wrap) to surface neglected areas. Also useful when Terry says "I feel stuck" or "something's off" but can't pinpoint it.

## The Areas

### 1. Health
**What healthy looks like:** Sleeping 7h+, exercising 2-3x/week, no deferred medical issues, meds taken on schedule.
**Neglect signals:** Skipping meals, caffeine after 2pm, deferring doctor appointments, sleep <6h for 3+ nights, sinusitis/back pain flaring without follow-up.
**Current systems:** Oura (sleep), sopor (tracking), Due reminders (meds), comes (nudges).

### 2. Family
**What healthy looks like:** Present with Theo daily (not just physically), connected with Tara beyond logistics, parents contacted regularly.
**Neglect signals:** Theo screen time creeping up, conversations with Tara only about scheduling, >2 weeks since calling parents, missing Theo milestones because of screen.
**Current systems:** None automated — this is the area that loses to "productive" work most often.

### 3. Money
**What healthy looks like:** Bills paid on time, savings on track, no surprise expenses, investment plan executing (not tinkering).
**Neglect signals:** Credit card balance carried, mortgage buffer <2 months, tax payment not reserved, overdue financial admin in TODO.md.
**Current systems:** fiscus (monthly review), Due reminders (bills), TODO.md recurring items.

### 4. Work / Craft
**What healthy looks like:** Engaged with meaningful problems, shipping real things, learning on the job, not just treading water.
**Neglect signals:** Going through motions, no "I learned X" moments in a week, avoiding hard problems, only doing admin/maintenance.
**Current systems:** rector (on-ramp), wrap (learning capture), weekly (synthesis).

### 5. Growth
**What healthy looks like:** Studying for GARP, reading substantively, building new capabilities, being challenged.
**Neglect signals:** GARP prep stalled >3 days, no non-work learning in a week, only consuming (podcasts, articles) without producing (notes, posts, experiments).
**Current systems:** dokime (quiz), melete (drill), garden (output forcing function).

### 6. Autonomy
**What healthy looks like:** Control over daily schedule, choosing what to work on, not reactive to others' urgency.
**Neglect signals:** Calendar full of others' meetings, working on things you didn't choose, "I have to" replacing "I want to", resentment building.
**Current systems:** kairos (priority check), fasti (calendar awareness).

### 7. Community / Belonging
**What healthy looks like:** Regular contact with friends, professional network alive, not isolated.
**Neglect signals:** >2 weeks since non-family social interaction, LinkedIn only for broadcasting (not engaging), no one to call if stressed, amicus showing stale contacts.
**Current systems:** amicus (contact staleness), theoros (LinkedIn feed), speculor (professional network).

### 8. Rest / Play
**What healthy looks like:** Genuine downtime that isn't productivity in disguise, weekends with Theo feel like weekends, hobbies exist.
**Neglect signals:** "Relaxing" means scrolling or optimising tools, can't remember last time you did something with no output, Sunday evening feels like Monday morning.
**Current systems:** quies (evening wind-down), mora (low-energy tasks — but this is still "productive rest", not real rest).

### 9. Contribution
**What healthy looks like:** Work connects to something larger than personal advancement, governance direction alive, 80,000 Hours aspiration held loosely.
**Neglect signals:** Pure mercenary mode for >1 month, no thought about impact beyond client/employer, garden posts only about tools (never about why).
**Current systems:** Garden (public thinking), career north star memory (governance direction).

## How to Use

**From kairos/daily/eow:** Quick scan — which area hasn't had attention in >1 week? Flag it, don't prescribe.

**When Terry says "something's off":** Walk through areas 1-9, ask which one resonates. Usually it's Family or Rest hiding behind a Work/Growth focus.

**Pattern to watch:** Terry's default failure mode is overinvesting in Work/Growth/Craft at the expense of Family/Rest/Community. The neglect is invisible because the productive areas feel good.

## Boundaries

- This is a diagnostic, not a scorecard. No tracking, no points, no weekly reviews of "life balance."
- Don't nag. Surface once, move on. Terry is an adult.
- Don't manufacture concern. If all areas look fine, say so.
- Financial constraint is real: some areas (contribution, autonomy) are gated by money. Acknowledge, don't minimise.
