## Lead (opus)
Holds the workshop arc. Understands the client context well enough to make judgment calls on which biopsy lenses matter most.
Produces the facilitation pack and owns the post-workshop synthesis.

## Workers (sonnet, parallel)
- **pre-work-researcher**: reads all available client artifacts — org charts, architecture diagrams, prior reports, public filings — and produces a 2-page context brief for the facilitator
- **facilitator-prep**: translates the context brief into a session-ready facilitation guide — agenda with time boxes, warm-up prompts, biopsy questions per lens, parking lot format
- **post-workshop-synthesizer**: takes raw session notes/transcripts and extracts: key tensions surfaced, decisions made, unresolved questions, follow-on commitments

## Protocol
1. Lead scopes: which biopsy lenses are active for this client? What outcome does the client need from the session?
2. Pre-work-researcher and facilitator-prep run in parallel — researcher feeds findings to prep worker as they emerge
3. Lead reviews the facilitation guide, adjusts for client sensitivities and session goals
4. Workshop runs (human-led, colony dormant)
5. Post-workshop: lead reactivates post-workshop-synthesizer with session notes
6. Synthesizer extracts findings, lead shapes into the synthesis doc
7. Colony dissolves

## Cost gate
~$3-5 total (split: ~$2 pre-work, ~$1-2 post-synthesis). Justified for any paid engagement. Internal team sessions = use a single bud for the agenda.

## Dissolution rule
Dissolves after the post-workshop synthesis doc is finalized. Colony does not persist between pre-workshop and post-workshop — reconstitute with fresh context from session notes.
