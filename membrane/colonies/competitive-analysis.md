---
name: competitive-analysis
description: Analyze competitor or peer approaches — each worker studies one, lead produces a comparative matrix with strategic implications.
product: competitive matrix + strategic brief with clear positioning implications
trigger: evaluating a market, validating a strategic bet, or preparing a client deliverable that requires competitive context
---

## Lead (opus)
Defines the evaluation dimensions before dispatching — what criteria matter for THIS decision?
Reads all worker reports with a single question: where does the subject have a defensible advantage or a dangerous blind spot?
Produces the matrix and the strategic brief. Does not hedge. States what the analysis implies.

## Workers (sonnet, parallel — one per competitor/peer)
Each worker studies one entity and produces a structured profile:
- What they do and how
- Where they are strong (evidence-based, not marketing)
- Where they are weak or absent
- What they are moving toward (signals, hiring, recent releases, public statements)
- One sentence: what would it take to beat them, or what they would need to beat the subject?

## Protocol
1. Lead defines the evaluation frame: who is being compared, against whom, for what decision
2. Lead specifies 4-6 evaluation dimensions (e.g., technical depth, go-to-market, pricing, speed, ecosystem)
3. Workers run in parallel — each receives the dimensions so their profiles are directly comparable
4. Workers produce structured profiles using the same schema
5. Lead reads all profiles and populates the comparative matrix
6. Lead writes the strategic brief: 3-5 implications, stated as direct claims not hedged observations
7. Lead flags where data was thin or where a competitor's trajectory is genuinely uncertain
8. Colony dissolves

## Cost gate
~$3-5 depending on number of competitors (scale: ~$0.50-1 per worker). Justified when: a real decision depends on the output (investment, pricing, positioning, client recommendation). Casual market awareness = single bud with web search, no colony needed.

## Dissolution rule
Dissolves after the strategic brief is written. The matrix alone is not the deliverable — implications are. A matrix without a strategic brief means the lead did not finish the job.
