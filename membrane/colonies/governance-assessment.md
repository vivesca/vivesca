## Lead (opus)
Frames the assessment lens before dispatching workers.
Resolves tension between dimensions (e.g., risk appetite vs. innovation pace).
Produces the final report: maturity scores, cross-cutting findings, 90-day roadmap.

## Workers (sonnet, parallel)
- **policy-lens**: governance policies, AI principles, acceptable use, model deployment approval chains, audit trails
- **technology-lens**: MLOps maturity, model versioning, monitoring, observability, incident playbooks
- **people-skills-lens**: AI literacy baseline, training gaps, role definitions, ownership accountability
- **data-lens**: data quality controls, lineage, consent and privacy compliance, data for AI governance specifically
- **risk-lens**: risk register for AI-specific failure modes, regulatory exposure, third-party model risk

## Protocol
1. Lead reads the client brief and scopes the assessment frame (sector, regulatory context, maturity baseline hypothesis)
2. Lead distributes assessment questions and evidence sources to each worker
3. Workers run in parallel — each interviews available artifacts (policies, org charts, tool inventories, risk registers)
4. Workers report: current state, maturity level (1-5), top 3 gaps, one quick win
5. Lead reads all five reports, identifies cross-cutting patterns (e.g., policy exists but technology doesn't enforce it)
6. Lead produces: executive summary, dimension scorecards, gap heat map, prioritized roadmap
7. Colony dissolves

## Cost gate
~$4-7 per assessment. Justified when: client is paying for a structured deliverable, one analyst would miss cross-dimension contradictions, or findings need to hold up in a board-level presentation. Internal quick-takes = single bud.

## Dissolution rule
Dissolves when the final report is written and verified by lead. Workers do not persist — each assessment is a fresh read.
