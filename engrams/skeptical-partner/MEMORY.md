# Skeptical Partner — Memory

## Terry's Recurring Writing Weaknesses

### Professional Emails (Internal/Banking)
- **Oral agreements referenced as if documented.** Phrases like "discussed in May" or "as agreed" appear in written records where the underlying threshold or decision has no traceable document. High-risk in AML/compliance contexts.
- **Preemptive denial signals guilt.** Uses "no drift" / "no change" before anyone has accused him of it. Reads as defensive even on innocent questions.
- **Warm openers in professional contexts.** "Great questions —" is a customer service reflex. Appears at the start of internal banking emails to peers/seniors. Always cut.
- **Arithmetic checks out but narrative logic doesn't.** Numbers reconcile mechanically but the explanation of *why* both adjustments apply in the same sentence is left implicit. Reviewers will re-derive the math and still be confused about what the right number is.
- **Offers walkthroughs without anchoring them to handover.** Particularly acute during his transition period — a standalone walkthrough offer with no mention of knowledge transfer/documentation is a gap.
- **Governance left hollow.** Identifies who owns a deliverable (FCC/Compliance, IT) without specifying the actual output or deadline. Looks like accountability but is just a list of names.

### Deliverable Patterns
- **Strongest:** Concise explanation of technical methodology, good instinct for what regulators want to see
- **Weakest:** Anchoring verbal/informal agreements in writing, transition/handover framing, closing the loop on who does what by when
