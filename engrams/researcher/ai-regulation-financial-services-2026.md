## Key Facts

### EU AI Act
- In force Aug 2024; high-risk compliance deadline Aug 2, 2026 (possibly extended to Dec 2027 via Digital Omnibus)
- Prohibited practices: from Feb 1, 2025
- Governance provisions: from Aug 2, 2025
- High-risk in banking: credit scoring, loan decisions, AML/fraud detection (credit scoring = explicitly named in Annex III; fraud detection = arguable exception under Annex III)
- Requirements for high-risk: quality management system, technical documentation, automatic logging, human oversight, conformity assessment, EU database registration, explainability to regulators + affected individuals
- Provider vs deployer: banks buying third-party AI are "deployers" — must do human oversight, monitoring, incident reporting
- Penalties: €35M or 7% global turnover (prohibited practices); €15M or 3% (high-risk obligations)
- National market surveillance authorities enforce; EBA will promote common supervisory approaches 2026-2027
- EBA factsheet Nov 2025: no contradiction with existing banking law; no immediate EBA guideline changes planned; sector already has complementary framework

### UK (PRA SS1/23 + FCA)
- SS1/23 effective May 17, 2024 — model risk management principles applying to ALL models including AI/ML
- PRA business plan 2025/26: focus on how banks are *embedding* SS1/23 — this is now implementation scrutiny, not rulemaking
- PRA Oct 2025 roundtable: 21 firms, CRO-level, discussed AI/ML adoption under SS1/23 — signal of active supervisory engagement
- Key SS1/23 AI requirements: explainability, data provenance, fairness, accountability; MRM as a risk discipline; proportionate to complexity
- FS2/23 (Oct 2023): AI/ML-specific supervisory statement — supplements SS1/23
- FCA: no AI-specific rules; principles-based (Consumer Duty, SM&CR, operational resilience); AI Live Testing cohort from Oct 2025
- FCA CEO Nikhil Rathi (Dec 2025): will not introduce AI rules; will intervene only for "egregious failures"
- Enforcement: FCA/PRA can use existing powers; no AI-specific fines yet but supervisory attention is live

### Hong Kong (HKMA)
- Foundation: 2019 Big Data/AI circular — governance, fairness, transparency, data privacy
- Aug 2024 GenAI circular: customer opt-out requirements, continuous monitoring of GenAI output, boards must establish oversight committees
- Sep 2024 AI/AML circular: banks must use AI for ML/TF monitoring; feasibility studies + implementation plans due by Mar 2025
- Oct 2024 cross-sector policy statement (FSTB): cybersecurity, data privacy, model transparency, financial stability
- SFC Nov 2024 circular: prescriptive shift — specific ops requirements for high-risk activities (investment advice): human-in-loop review, prompt variation testing, SFC notification of model changes
- Nov 2025 Fintech 2030 / AI2 strategy: DART framework, 40+ measures, shared AI infrastructure, finance-specific models
- Mar 2026 GenAI Sandbox++: multi-sector (banking, securities, insurance, MPF), emphasis on AI governance embedding in 3 lines of defence, "AI vs AI" frameworks
- HKMA enforcement: circulars are supervisory guidance; enforcement via HKMA supervisory powers (Banking Ordinance); no AI-specific fines publicly reported

### Singapore (MAS)
- FEAT principles: 2018 (Fairness, Ethics, Accountability, Transparency)
- Veritas framework: 2019–2022, operationalizes FEAT for credit, insurance, customer marketing
- Consultation Paper on AI Risk Management Guidelines: Nov 13, 2025 — consultation closed Jan 31, 2026
- Status: finalizing guidelines; 12-month transition period after issuance (likely effective late 2026 or early 2027)
- Key requirements: AI inventory, board/senior mgmt oversight, lifecycle controls, proportionality (scales with risk materiality), explainability for high-impact decisions, human oversight for high-risk deployments, third-party AI risk management
- Enforcement: MAS supervisory powers; penalties not specified in consultation paper
- Significance: FEAT was principles only; new AIRM Guidelines are the first operationalized, binding-level MAS expectations for AI governance

### United States
- SR 11-7 (Fed/OCC 2011): foundation model risk management guidance — applies to AI models
- OCC 2025 bulletin: clarification for community banks on MRM — scalable, principles-based
- Trump Jan 2025 EO: rescinded Biden EO 14110; deregulatory stance; "removing barriers"
- Trump Dec 2025 EO: national framework preempting state AI laws; minimize federal burden
- CFPB 2025: rolled back disparate impact enforcement for fair lending AI
- State level: filling gap — Massachusetts $2.5M settlement for AI disparate impact
- GAO 2025 report: flagged fragmented oversight, no single AI-in-finance regulator
- No new AI-specific banking regulation expected under current administration
- SR 11-7 remains the backbone — OCC confirmed AI falls within scope
- Fed AI review: conducted AI-focused reviews of 7 large banks 2019-2023, made recommendations (not public)

### International (BIS / FSB / OECD)
- FSB Oct 2025 report: monitoring adoption + vulnerabilities; no new rules; encourages national authorities to improve monitoring
- BIS Dec 2024 paper: regulatory challenges, data gaps, concentration risk from third-party AI providers
- OECD Jan 2026 report "Supervision of AI in Finance": principles for supervisory approaches
- G20 Oct 2025: BIS + FSB submitted joint AI monitoring paper
- BCBS: no AI-specific Basel standard; existing credit risk/operational risk framework applies
- Main themes: regulatory fragmentation, lack of harmonized definitions, concentration risk from large AI vendors (OpenAI, Google, Microsoft), herding behavior risk

## Key Trends
1. **Principles → enforcement**: EU is moving hardest (Aug 2026 deadline with teeth). UK is at "supervisory engagement" (SS1/23 implementation scrutiny). US is DEREGULATING at federal level but states are filling the gap.
2. **HSBC pressure points**: EU AI Act (EU operations, credit scoring must register as high-risk Aug 2026), UK PRA SS1/23 (implementation scrutiny now, live), HKMA (circulars are guidance but HKMA relationship supervision is real), MAS (AIRM guidelines incoming 2026-2027). HSBC is the highest-pressure multi-jurisdiction case.
3. **Fraud detection carve-out**: EU AI Act Annex III originally listed credit scoring as high-risk but fraud detection as a possible exception. EBA Nov 2025 confirms ambiguity remains.
4. **Third-party AI risk**: All jurisdictions now explicitly include third-party AI in scope (vendor AI = bank's responsibility).
5. **US deregulation creates arbitrage risk**: Global banks face risk of EU/UK/APAC compliance drag while US competitors face lighter touch.

## HSBC-Specific Multi-Jurisdiction Map
- UK: PRA SS1/23 + FCA Consumer Duty — ACTIVE supervisory scrutiny, most pressing near-term
- EU: AI Act high-risk compliance by Aug 2026 — hard legal deadline, penalty risk
- HK: HKMA circulars + AI2 strategy — relationship-based supervision, GenAI governance emphasis
- SG: MAS AIRM guidelines — final guidelines 2026, 12-month transition → compliance due ~2027
- US: Deregulatory but SR 11-7 backbone remains; state laws a patchwork risk
