## Key Verified Facts

### BN(O) Visa
- Requires: Must hold BN(O) status (registered before 1997 handover) OR child of BN(O) holder
- As of Feb 9, 2026: adult children born before July 1, 1997 can now apply independently too
- No job offer required. Work freely in any sector (except professional sports)
- Fees: £193 (2.5yr) or £268 (5yr) per person + IHS (Immigration Health Surcharge) £1,035/yr
- ILR: 5 years continuous residence → apply for ILR (£3,029/person), 6-month processing
- Citizenship: 1 year after ILR → total 6 years from arrival
- BN(O) PROTECTED from "earned settlement" 10-year extension — retains 5-year ILR pathway
- Family: dependants (spouse + children) can be included on same application

### Skilled Worker Visa (SOC codes for this profile)
- SOC 2433 (Actuaries/Economists/Statisticians — includes "Statistical data scientists"): going rate £55,100/yr
- SOC 2431 (Management Consultants and Business Analysts): going rate £50,200/yr
- SOC 2139 (IT Professionals NEC — includes "IT consultants"): going rate £52,300/yr
- SOC 2134 (Programmers/Software Development): going rate £54,700/yr
- General threshold (Jul 22 2025 onwards): £41,700/yr or going rate, whichever higher
- Points: 70 required. Job offer from licensed sponsor = 20pts + salary threshold = 20pts + appropriate skill level = 20pts + English B2+ = 10pts
- Skill level raised to RQF Level 6 from Jan 2026 (reduces eligible roles by ~180 but data science/consulting not affected)
- English: B2 required from Jan 8, 2026 for new applicants
- Capco is a Wipro subsidiary — as a global professional services firm with active London operations, extremely likely to be a licensed sponsor; verifiable at gov.uk register of licensed sponsors

### Senior or Specialist Worker (Intra-Company Transfer)
- Replaces old Tier 2 ICT; requires 12 months employment with same corporate group (overseas) unless earning £73,900+
- Minimum salary: £52,500 or going rate (whichever higher)
- Does NOT lead to ILR (no settlement pathway — key disadvantage vs Skilled Worker)
- Visa fees: £769 (up to 3yr) or £1,519 (over 3yr) outside UK + IHS £1,035/yr

### Global Talent Visa (Digital Technology via Tech Nation)
- No job offer required; no employer sponsor required
- Two tracks: "Exceptional Talent" (established leader) or "Exceptional Promise" (potential leader)
- Need 2+ of 5 criteria: innovation as founder/exec, innovation as employee, contributions outside work, technical/commercial/entrepreneurial contributions, published/expert-endorsed research
- Endorsement fee: £561; visa fee: £205; IHS £1,035/yr
- ILR: 3 years (Talent track) or 5 years (Promise track)
- Tech Nation renewed as endorsing body May 2025 (3-year contract)
- From Aug 4, 2025: single GOV.UK Stage 1 form replaces Tech Nation's own form
- Success rate: 54–65% at endorsement stage for digital tech; 85%+ at visa stage post-endorsement
- Priority processing for AI/cybersecurity applications: 3 weeks instead of 8

### "Earned Settlement" Policy Changes (Nov 2025 White Paper)
- General change: ILR baseline moving from 5 years to 10 years for most migrants
- HIGH-SKILL carve-out: £50,270+ earners OR public sector healthcare/teaching → remain at 5 years
- BN(O) carve-out: BN(O) holders explicitly protected — retain 5-year pathway
- Implementation: changes begin April 2026 but consultation closed Feb 12, 2026 — transitional arrangements TBD
- Skilled Worker at £50,270+: likely still qualifies for 5-year ILR under high-skill track

### Credential Recognition
- HKICPA → ICAEW: reciprocal membership agreement (RMA), valid Nov 2023–Nov 2028
  - HKICPA QP completers: join ICAEW without further exams
  - Cannot practice audit without separate Audit Qualification (AQ)
  - Active for 2+ years before being allowed to practise
- CIA: globally recognised by IIA; no conversion needed — the CIA is already a global credential
- Both credentials remain valid and recognised by UK employers in finance/consulting

### London vs HK Cost of Living
- Overall: London is approximately 17–34% more expensive than Hong Kong (varies by methodology)
- Rent: London is 17.9% more expensive than HK on average, but central London significantly higher
- London rent: 1-bed city centre ~£2,200–£2,800/month; 2-bed ~£3,000–£4,000
- HK rent: 1-bed central ~HK$23,500 (~£2,300); 2-bed ~HK$27,700–40,000 (~£2,700–£3,900)
- Tax: UK income tax rates — 20% (£12,571–£50,270), 40% (£50,271–£125,140), 45% above. Plus NIC.
  - HK income tax: flat, capped at 15% effective. Massive difference at senior salary levels.
- Healthcare: UK NHS (free) vs HK private (employer-provided typical for professionals)
- Childcare: significantly more expensive in London

## Misinformation Patterns Encountered
- Some sites conflate "Tier 2 ICT" (old name) with "Senior or Specialist Worker" — these are the same route now renamed
- "Senior or Specialist Worker leads to ILR" is incorrect — it does NOT
- Tech Nation closed/contract situation created confusion; as of May 2025, Tech Nation is confirmed renewed
- "Earned settlement = 10 years for everyone" — wrong, there are carve-outs for high earners and BN(O) holders
- Corporate transfer visa (SSW) is often misreported as equivalent to Skilled Worker for settlement purposes — key difference is SSW has NO ILR pathway
