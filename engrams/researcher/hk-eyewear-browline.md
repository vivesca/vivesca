## Key Brand/Model Facts
- MOSCOT TINIF: only true browline in Moscot's prescription lineup (metal brow + acetate lower), 48mm only, HK$2,988 — fits the brief exactly
- MOSCOT LEMTOSH: square acetate, NOT a browline. Common confusion.
- Oliver Peoples Gregory Peck (OV5186): full acetate round/pantos, NOT a true browline. "Shulman" (OV1177) is closer to browline but full-rim metal.
- Persol PO3092V: thick browline + open lower, sizes 48/50mm, has the Meflecto flex temple (comfort benefit), but fixed plastic nose pads — NOT adjustable. NOT ideal for Asian fit.
- Ray-Ban RX3916VF: Clubmaster Square Asian Fit — has adjustable nose pads, prescription-ready. More square profile than classic RX5154. Only 55mm — too large.
- Lunor M9: titanium minimalist — NOT browline. Browline styles are in Lunor A-series acetate.
- 2nd Frame (2ndframe.com): Taiwan brand, browline collection online, NO Hong Kong physical store confirmed.
- A. Society (Hong Kong brand): no browline frames — bold statement silhouettes, not classic styles.

## Asian Fit / Nose Pad Summary
- Best for low nose bridge: Moscot TINIF (metal nose pads, confirmed), LEMTOSH W/ Nose Pads (alt fit, 46/49mm)
- Fixed plastic nose pads (not adjustable): Persol PO3092V — borderline, can cause slipping on low bridges
- Adjustable metal nose pads confirmed: Ray-Ban RX3016/RX3916VF Clubmaster variants
- Oliver Peoples acetate frames: no adjustable nose pads; OV5186A low bridge fit has repositioned bridge but still acetate
- MOSCOT metal nose pad review note: one reviewer found metal pads MORE slippery than acetate despite better bridge clearance
