# LinkedIn Comment Networking Patterns

Captured from Gary Tsoi comment session (Feb 24, 2026). 4 iterations, 3 consilium runs.

## Seniority-Aware Tone

When commenting on a **more senior** person's post:
- **"This really resonates"** > "Well put" > "Spot on" — first appreciates, second and third evaluate from above
- **Never grade their thinking** — "that's the part most exec teams skip past" implies you're qualified to judge
- **Share experience as observation**, not authority — "I've seen X up close" > "I've been on the receiving end of X" (victim framing) > "I've seen too many X" (consultant cynicism)

## Engagement Strategy

- **Engage with the non-obvious point**, not the headline — every other commenter will react to the obvious hook (e.g. a named framework). Picking the sharper, less obvious argument shows you actually read it.
- **Echo their language** — using the author's own words/phrases signals close reading (e.g. "determines if it scales" when they wrote "platforms scale")
- **No forced questions at the end** — formulaic "coaching questions" read as LinkedIn engagement tactics. If a question doesn't pass the "would I ask this over coffee?" test, skip it.
- **Let your profile headline do the credential work** — don't inject your title/expertise into the comment. When someone writes something sharp, people click through to see who they are.

## Council Insights

- **Quick mode ($0.10-0.20) is sufficient for comment reviews** after establishing ground rules in the first round. Full council ($0.60) was valuable for the initial strategic framing but overkill for subsequent iterations.
- **Over-polishing trap**: iterative de-risking produces safe but boring comments. After 2 rounds of refinement, ask "is this boring?" before "is this safe?"
- **Separate public engagement from private positioning** — the comment builds rapport and visibility; the DM (if they reply) is where you mention your role/suggest coffee.

## HK Banking Specifics

- **Don't name their employer** in public comments asking about internal initiatives — compliance sensitivity in HK banking culture
- **"Buy the platform" is a universally relatable critique** — safe to use because it criticises a pattern, not a person
- **10-12 month consulting sales cycles** mean early networking touchpoints are strategically valuable, not premature
