# Pattern: Conversation Cards

**Problem:** Vault notes organized by *source* (when/where captured) are hard to retrieve by *use case* (when/where needed). Before a meeting, you need three talking points — not ten links to explore.

**Solution:** Thin retrieval-optimized cards that sit above deep notes. One card per topic you regularly discuss. Card links back to source notes for depth; source notes stay organized by origin.

## Card Structure

1. **Three points** — each with one killer example
2. **Thread** — one sentence that ties all three together
3. **Audience calibration table** — which point to lead with for CTO vs CRO vs CEO vs Engineering
4. **Ammo** — drop-in data points (numbers, dates, names) you can cite without looking anything up
5. **Source notes** — links back to deep notes for follow-up conversations

## Key Disciplines

- **30-second scan.** If it takes longer, the card is too long.
- **Cap at ~5 cards.** More than that and you've rebuilt the navigation problem you were solving.
- **Cards don't duplicate content.** Three points + examples + links. Deep analysis stays in source notes.
- **Refresh weekly.** Added to `/weekly` Friday Reset Checklist (step 5). 60 seconds per card — check data points, update if source notes changed.
- **Different from a MOC.** MOC = "here's where everything lives" (navigation). Card = "here's what to say and to whom" (performance). If a card starts accumulating links and growing past one page, it's becoming a MOC — trim it.

## When to Create a Card

- You've discussed the topic 2+ times in different contexts
- The material exists across 3+ vault notes organized by source
- You need to speak about it with calibration to different audiences

## Anti-Patterns

- Card > 1 page → trim or it becomes a MOC
- Card with no audience calibration → it's just a summary, not a conversation tool
- Card without source links → can't go deeper in follow-up conversations
- Updating facts in the card but not the source note → drift
