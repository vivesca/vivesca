# LinkedIn Algorithm 2026 — What's Working

Last updated: 2026-02-28. Sources: Sprout Social, Dataslayer, Postiv AI (2M+ posts).

## Hard Rules

- **No external links in post body** — 60% reach penalty. Put URL in comments.
- **First 60 minutes is everything** — only 5% of underperforming posts recover after the first hour.
- **800–1000 characters** is the sweet spot for text posts.
- **Hook must work above the fold** — first 1–2 lines before "see more" determine whether anyone reads.

## Format Performance (engagement rate)

| Format | Engagement |
|---|---|
| Document / PDF carousel | 6.6% (best) |
| Polls | 1.64x reach multiplier |
| Text only | ~2% |
| Video (native) | High reach, lower engagement |
| Posts with external links | −60% reach |

## What's Working in 2026

- **Document posts** (PDF carousels) are the highest-performing format — worth converting key posts to carousel
- **Shorter posts** — trend toward concise, not long-form
- **Individual > company page** — employee reshares reach 561% further than company posts
- **Dwell time matters** — LinkedIn measures how long people actually read, not just clicks/likes

## Comment Algorithm Rules

- **15+ words = ~2x engagement weight** — LinkedIn algorithmically rewards substantive comments over short ones
- **Golden hour applies to comments too** — commenting on a post >24h old has minimal distribution impact; >48h is effectively dead for discovery
- **LinkedIn detects and penalises engagement bait** ("Comment YES if you agree!") — penalised at algorithm level, not just socially

## Post Strategy for Consilium / Tool Posts

1. Write text + attach screenshot/image (no link in body)
2. Strong single-sentence hook above the fold
3. Keep to ~800 chars
4. Link to site in first comment
5. Engage with comments in first 60 min to feed the algorithm
