# Office Noise, Focus Music & Acoustic Strategy

Research-backed notes from March 2026 (pplx deep research + WebSearch).

## Core Finding: Habituate vs. Mask

**Don't try to habituate to speech noise.** It's not an attention problem — it's phonological interference. Your auditory cortex involuntarily tracks intelligible speech in your native language at the neural level. No amount of willpower or exposure overcomes this. Productivity drops up to two-thirds when exposed to intelligible colleague conversations (open-plan office research, multiple studies).

Habituation is also physiologically incomplete for low-frequency noise: cortisol and cardiovascular stress markers remain elevated even after subjective adaptation.

**Verdict:** Masking > habituation. Use noise-blocking or masking as infrastructure, not willpower.

## Noise Colour Research (2023–2025)

| Colour | Findings | Best for |
|--------|----------|----------|
| White (~45 dB) | Best-studied; consistent attention + accuracy benefits | General focus |
| Pink | Small benefits for ADHD populations (stochastic resonance); slight impairment for neurotypical | ADHD/attention issues |
| Brown | Perceptually preferred (less unease, more "sublimity"), less fatiguing — but **no direct cognitive performance studies yet exist** | Sustained comfort |

**Key caveat:** Brown noise hype has outrun the science. The benefit is likely real (masking speech frequencies) but the colour-specific mechanism is unproven. White noise at 45 dB has the most evidence.

**Optimal volume:** ~45–65 dB. Above 70 dB the masking itself causes fatigue.

## Individual vs. Office-Level Interventions

- **Adaptive sound masking** = infrastructure (ceiling-mounted speakers, building/facilities level). 2025 Eindhoven study: 20–30% improvement in focus/task accuracy, reduced need for personal headphones.
- **Personal headphones + noise colour** = the individual-level equivalent.
- Not interchangeable — adaptive masking is for office managers, not individuals.

## Music Strategy by Task Type

### Deep/verbal work (writing, analysis, coding logic, client comms)
- Brown noise or white noise only — no music
- Dark ambient acceptable (formless, non-melodic, no rhythm to lock onto)
- **No lofi** — rhythmic/melodic patterns compete with verbal working memory

### Routine/mechanical work (email, formatting, structured data)
- Instrumental lofi works well — occupies the latching part of your brain without interference
- Dark ambient also fine
- **No vocals in any music** — phonological interference applies even to background vocals

### Layering
- Lofi *is* the masking — no need to layer brown noise underneath
- Layering both is fine for very loud offices but redundant; lofi drowns out brown noise anyway
- Keep combined volume under ~65 dB

## Dark Ambient for Deep Work

Arguably the most cognitively inert background music — deliberately formless, non-melodic, no rhythmic pulse. Low-frequency drones overlap spectrally with brown noise.

**Starting points:**
- Brian Eno — *Ambient 1: Music for Airports* (1978) — the genre origin; 40 min, immediately tells you if it works
- Brian Eno — *Ambient 2: The Plateaux of Mirror* (1980, with Harold Budd) — warmer, crystalline
- Brian Eno — *Ambient 4: On Land* (1982) — darker, field recordings + drones
- Brian Eno — *Apollo: Atmospheres and Soundtracks* (1983) — expansive, accessible
- Lustmord, Atrium Carceri — darker end of the spectrum
- YouTube/Spotify: "dark ambient focus" for curated playlists
- [💻 founder mode | dark ambient for building at 3am](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NCrui_dYJY) — sub-bass drones, synth pads, no vocals; unverified but description matches the profile

**Caveat:** Some find extended dark ambient mood-depressing. Highly personal — trial required.

## Lofi Specifically

- Best source: **Lofi Girl** (YouTube/Spotify) — almost entirely instrumental, the genre benchmark
- **College Music** on YouTube — similar quality
- Spotify: "lofi instrumental study"
- Why it works: deliberately low-fidelity and repetitive → brain files it as background, doesn't reward active listening
- Why jazz/classical doesn't work the same way: dynamic variation invites attention

## Capco Context

Consulting firm = lots of English speech, open plan, phone calls. Speech noise is the primary threat.

Practical setup:
1. Over-ear headphones (social signal: "I'm heads down")
2. Brown/white noise or dark ambient for focus blocks
3. Lofi instrumental for routine tasks
4. Use quiet zones / focus rooms for the deepest work
5. Flag to facilities if acoustic environment is persistently bad (adaptive masking is the infrastructure fix)
