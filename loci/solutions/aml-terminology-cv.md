# AML Terminology in CVs and Client-Facing Documents

## "Suppressed" is a red flag

In AML/FinCrime compliance, "suppressing alerts" implies hiding suspicious activity from regulators — literally a compliance violation. Multiple council models flagged this independently.

**Don't use:** "suppressed", "suppression strategy"
**Use instead:** "hibernation" (industry term), "deprioritised", "classified as low-risk"

## "Hibernation" is the correct AML term

Alert hibernation = setting aside low-risk alerts in a dormant state (retrievable if needed, not deleted). Recognized term in transaction monitoring. HSBC FCC/AML staff will understand it immediately.

## Framing matters: "30% of alerts" vs "30% of low-risk alerts"

- "Hibernation of 30% of low-risk alerts" → implies only some low-risk alerts were hibernated
- "30% of alerts identified as low-risk for hibernation" → all low-risk alerts hibernated, and they comprise 30% of total volume

The second framing is correct when the model classified 30% of all alerts as low-risk and all of those went to hibernation.
