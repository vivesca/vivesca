# Financial Messages Through Intermediaries

## Pattern (Feb 2026, from 2 council rounds)

When sending a financial figure that will be relayed through an intermediary (recruiter, broker, etc.):

1. **Split components** — don't bundle into one ambiguous number. "80k including tax" can mean "80k total" or "80k + tax on top." The intermediary will simplify when forwarding.
2. **Use a ceiling, not a range** — decision-makers approve budgets, not bands. "Around 85k" is better than "80-85k" (they'll approve the floor).
3. **Specify whose tax and what type** — "tax" alone is ambiguous in corporate contexts. "Grossed up for my income tax" is five words that remove all confusion.
4. **Make it copy-pasteable** — the intermediary will forward your message or paraphrase it. Give them numbers they can relay accurately.

## Anti-pattern

> "Roughly 80k including tax" → Gavin forwards "Terry says 80k" → COO approves 80k → invoice comes at 74k + 13k tax = 87k → looks like a bait-and-switch

## Better

> "The buyout is roughly 75k, and around 85k grossed up for tax" → Gavin forwards "75k buyout, 85k total cost" → COO approves 85k → everyone aligned
