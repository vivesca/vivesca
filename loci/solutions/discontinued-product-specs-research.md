# Finding Specs for Discontinued Products

## Pattern

When a product is fully discontinued (removed from manufacturer site, delisted from Shopify API, never archived by Wayback Machine), **third-party replacement part suppliers** often retain the original specs because they need exact dimensions to sell compatible parts.

## Example: ROKA Evans Eyeglasses

- Product page: 404
- Shopify API: delisted
- Wayback Machine: never crawled
- Google/Amazon: zero results
- **Sunglass Fix (replacement lens provider): confirmed 49mm lens width**

## Search Order for Discontinued Products

1. Manufacturer site / API (usually gone)
2. Wayback Machine / Google cache (often not archived)
3. Perplexity with specific model + specs query (aggregates across replacement part sites)
4. Third-party replacement part suppliers (lenses, batteries, filters, etc.)
5. eBay/secondhand listings (sometimes include original specs in description)
6. Order confirmation emails (if you bought it)
7. Physical product markings (last resort)

## Key Insight

Replacement part suppliers are the most reliable source for discontinued product specs because their business depends on exact dimensional accuracy. They persist long after the original manufacturer removes listings.
