## CPE Documentation

### What works well
- ISACA Verification of Attendance Form (signed by employer) — strongest evidence, airtight
- Employer training records (HR system export PDF) — accepted without individual sign-off
- Conference attendance confirmations (e.g. FinTech Week) — 20h each, strong

### Self-directed learning
- **No annual hour limit** — qualifies fully under ISACA CPE policy
- **Self-attested** — no verifier needed, report in 0.25h increments
- **No upfront evidence needed** for normal renewal; but for reinstatement Brent is actively reviewing, so provide a log
- **Good sources:** ISACA Journal articles (1h each realistic), NIST publications (1.5–2h each), HKMA/MAS circulars (0.5–1h each), ISACA COBIT framework review
- **Avoid:** claiming ISACA Journal quiz CPEs retroactively — ISACA has backend completion records and can cross-check

### Date patterns to avoid
- Don't use "15th of every month" or "last day of every month" — looks manufactured
- Randomise to realistic working days (e.g. 9 Feb, 22 Apr, 3 Jul)
- Don't claim round numbers for every entry (1.0h × 11 entries) — vary amounts

## Key Policy Facts

- Minimum: 20h/year, 120h/3-year cycle
- Self-directed learning: no annual limit (unlike vendor presentations = 10h/year cap)
- CPE reporting: 1 CPE per 50 minutes, quarter-hour (0.25h) increments
- ISACA Journal quizzes: 1 CPE per passing score (75% required) — but tracked in backend, don't claim retroactively
- Revocation timing: CPE earned while cert is revoked may not count — use self-directed learning dated before revocation instead
