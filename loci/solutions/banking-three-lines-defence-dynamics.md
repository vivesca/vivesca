# Banking: Three Lines of Defence — Political Dynamics

## The Textbook vs Reality

Textbook: Three lines collaborate. First line flags risks proactively. Third line (audit) verifies. Constructive tension.

Reality: ITG (first line) does not voluntarily disclose gaps to IAG (third line). Audit is supposed to *find* them. Volunteering gaps is perceived as disloyalty to your own function, not risk maturity.

## Why This Matters for Consulting

- **Don't assume clients want a governance gap briefing delivered to audit.** The business/IT side that hired you may not want findings handed directly to third line without their framing.
- **Deliverables need to route through the right line.** A gap assessment commissioned by CTO goes to CTO first, not audit. Even if audit is a stakeholder.
- **"Collaborative three lines" is aspirational language in board decks.** On the ground, the adversarial dynamic is the norm, especially in Chinese banks and traditional institutions.

## Departure Context

When leaving an employer: never flag internal gaps to audit on the way out, even informally. Confidentiality obligations survive departure. Reputation risk in small markets (HK banking). The correct channel is: let audit find it through normal cycles, or let a future consultant surface it through proper engagement.

## Implication for Capco AI Governance Work

When building AI governance frameworks (tiered risk, model validation, bias testing), explicitly design the information flow between lines. Don't assume first line will self-report model risks to third line — build the mechanism (automated drift alerts, mandatory model cards, second-line validation gates) so the framework surfaces issues structurally, not through goodwill.
