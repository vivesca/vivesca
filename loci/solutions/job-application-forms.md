## Format: Structured Form vs Standalone Free-Text

- **Structured form** (each role has its own description field): Use bullet-point version with `- ` prefix. Problem/Solution/Governance/Impact structure works well.
- **Standalone free-text** (single box, no other context): Needs framing bookends — opener paragraph (who you are) + credentials closer (CPA, CIA, prior experience). The gist's prose version covers this.

## Non-Compete Questions

Standard employment contracts and signed offers elsewhere are NOT non-competes. Only answer "Yes" if there's an actual restrictive covenant (non-compete clause, non-solicit, conflict of interest provision). Capco offer = not a non-compete.

## Comp Anchoring — "Upgrade or Pass" Situations

**Salary reference (CNCBI, confirmed Feb 2026):** HKD 99,500/month base. Employer provident fund: 10% of salary (HKD 9,950/month), employer-only, 10-year vesting. Total package ~HKD 109,450/month. Use as floor anchor for future negotiations.

When you already have an accepted offer, anchor comp expectations at what it would take to switch, not at the accepted offer's number. Underbidding to "get in the door" is pointless if you wouldn't accept the resulting offer.

For Director-level at large FIs/insurers: 1.5-1.8M total comp is the safe range — serious without pricing out. For roles where budget is genuinely unknown, add "depending on bonus structure and scope."

## Form Fatigue — Easy Wins That Get Missed

Certificates (CPA, CIA), languages (Cantonese/English/Mandarin), education details (degree type, field of study) — all get skipped on first pass. Keep the gist as a checklist.

## Skills Deduplication

Workday-style forms accept comma-separated skills. Maintain a single deduplicated list in the gist. Common duplicates: Machine Learning, Risk, Governance, Control.

## Post-Capco Application Strategy

Not job hunting — opportunistically screening. Threshold: must be a genuine upgrade (step-change in scope/title/trajectory), not marginal comp improvement. Backing out of Capco burns a bridge. Limit to 2-3 well-targeted applications, not broad spray.

Timing risk: large insurers/banks move slowly. An offer arriving after Capco start date is worse than backing out before starting.
