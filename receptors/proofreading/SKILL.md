---
name: proofreading
description: Stress-test an idea, plan, or claim by finding the strongest counterarguments and failure modes
user-invocable: true
context: fork
---

Take the given idea and attack it. Find the strongest counterarguments, not the most obvious ones. Look for hidden assumptions, failure modes, contradicting evidence, and boundary conditions. Be adversarial but honest — don't manufacture objections. If the idea holds up, say so. Present the three strongest challenges ranked by severity.

The nudge: forces adversarial mode when baseline would be agreeable. Baseline tends to validate ideas; this skill explicitly pushes against that bias.
